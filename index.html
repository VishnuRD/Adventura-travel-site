Adventure in Rishikesh

Rishikesh is yet another kickass destination for kayaking in India. The 12-km expedition begins at Brahmapuri and ends at Rishikesh’s popular ghat. The heavier rapids that you’d come across would surely pump up your adrenaline rush and keep you energized for the whole journey. What makes this place special is that it offers much more to sight than jus nature. The ashrams and temples oozing bliss are amongst the top places to spot on your way there. Kayaking in Rishikesh is an absolutely thrilling experience. Location: Brahmapuri, Rishikesh, India Best time to visit: March to early-May (September to mid-November for rafting)

A week long homestay hiking option in Western Himalayas

It’s great to hear that you have considerable experience of hiking. It will certainly give you more options to explore the possibilities. During mid April, you may consider all the winter/spring trek routes like Kedarkantha, Har Ki Dun, Winter Kuari Pass and few others. Note that these are popular routes and may have other groups visiting around the same time. Campsites are limited even if you are on a private tour. Alternatively, you may consider Bedni Bugyal trek or Dodital trek (possible to add with Dayara Bugyal) which are less frequent during your travel time.
